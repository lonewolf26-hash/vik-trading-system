02 — The Ideal Setup
02 What the Perfect Short Setup Looks Like
  • ✅
    Weak or no catalyst — vague PR, name change, S-8 filing, $190K crypto gains, non-binding MOU, reverse split for compliance. NOT real earnings, FDA approvals, or major contracts.
  • ✅
    Low float — under 10M shares ideal, under 20M acceptable. Thinner float = bigger moves but also harder to borrow.
  • ✅
    Extreme relative volume — 50x+ average is ideal. 10–50x is acceptable. Under 10x = skip it.
  • ✅
    Already extended — up 50–200%+ from prior close. The more extended, the better the short thesis. Wait for exhaustion, not the peak.
  • ✅
    Low short interest — under 15% of float is ideal. High short interest (25%+) = squeeze risk = avoid or size very small.
  • ✅
    Borrow available — confirmed on IBKR before entry. No borrow = no trade, no exceptions.
  • ✅
    Showing exhaustion — rejected HOD, lower highs on 1-min, volume drying up on pushes, first red candle after multiple greens.
Real examples of ideal setups: RMSG (no catalyst, 195x volume), AEHL ($190K Bitcoin gains, 406x volume), JZXN ($210K crypto gains, 1338x volume), SNYR (S-8 filing, 642x volume)

04 — Pre-Trade Checklist
04 Run This Before Every Single Entry
  • Catalyst identified and rated WEAK or MIXED — not strong
  • Float confirmed under 20M shares
  • Relative volume confirmed 10x+ average
  • Short interest confirmed under 20% of float
  • Borrow checked on IBKR — available at acceptable rate
  • Market cap above $5M — no sub-$5M market cap shorts (WOK lesson)
  • Stock is NOT in active halt right now
  • No binary catalyst upcoming (earnings today, FDA today)
  • Entry is at HOD rejection zone — NOT chasing a new high
  • Stop loss level identified and ready to set immediately on fill
  • Position size calculated — max loss does not exceed $150
  • Not trading the day after a big loss — emotional state clear
05 — Entry Rules
05 When and How to Enter
  • 📍
    Wait for HOD rejection — Never short into a new high. Wait for the stock to tag HOD, show a red candle below it, then enter. The failed breakout is your signal.
  • 📍
    Look for lower highs — On the 1-min chart, each push to HOD should be weaker than the last (lower volume, smaller candle range). Two failed HOD tests = strong entry signal.
  • 📍
    Volume drying up — Volume should be declining on upside pushes. If volume is still surging, momentum is still with bulls — wait.
  • 📍
    First red candle after green run — After 3–5 consecutive green 1-min candles, the first red close is your entry trigger. Don't wait for a second confirmation.
  • 📍
    Never short during a halt — If stock is halted, wait for resume. Assess direction on the first post-halt candle before entering.
  • 📍
    Never chase a fade already in progress — If the stock is already down 20%+ from HOD when you notice it, the trade has passed. Don't enter late.
The Entry Pattern You're Looking For
Stock pushes to HOD → gets rejected → pulls back → attempts HOD again on lower volume → fails to make new high → prints red candle → ENTER SHORT HERE

07 — Stop Loss Rules
07 Stop Loss — Non-Negotiable Rules
  • 🛑
    Set immediately on fill — The FIRST thing you do after getting filled is set your stop. Not after checking Twitter. Not after looking at another ticker. IMMEDIATELY.
  • 🛑
    Stop goes above HOD — Typically HOD + 10–15% gives it room. If HOD is $1.50, stop at $1.70–$1.75.
  • 🛑
    Never move stop higher — If price approaches your stop, it means the thesis is wrong. Cover, don't move the stop.
  • 🛑
    Honor the stop — no exceptions — RMSG cost ~$100. WOK cost a big loss because the stop wasn't honored. The stop exists to prevent catastrophic losses.
  • 🛑
    Halt resume rule — If stock halts while you're short and resumes significantly above your stop, EXIT immediately on the first tradeable price. Don't wait for a pullback.
The WOK Rule
WOK went from $1.40 entry to $10 before crashing to $1.20. The thesis was right — stock went to $1.20 eventually. But without a hard stop, the intermediate squeeze caused a massive loss. A correct thesis doesn't prevent losses if the stop isn't honored.
08 — Exit Rules & Targets
08 When and How to Exit
Target 1 — Scale Out
Cover 50% of position at first major support level or prior day's close. Lock in partial profit and let the rest ride.
Target 2 — Full Exit
Cover remaining at next support level, today's open price, or LOD. Don't wait for a full collapse — take the profit.
Stop Hit
Cover immediately, full position. No averaging up on a losing short. Move to the next trade.
  • 🕐
    3:30 PM hard cut — Never hold a micro-cap short into the close. Covers at 3:30 PM regardless of P&L. After-hours action on these names is unpredictable.
  • 🕐
    Expensive borrow = intraday only — If borrow rate is above 100% APR, this is a same-day trade only. Calculate daily cost before entering.
  • 🕐
    Trail the stop on winning trades — Once down 20% from entry, move stop to breakeven. Once down 30%, trail stop to lock in profit.
09 — Halt Management
09 What to Do When a Halt Happens

LULD (Limit Up-Limit Down) halts on parabolic stocks are common. On stocks moving 100%+ in a day, expect multiple halts. They typically last 5 minutes.

Halted against you
Watch bid/ask during halt. If bid is holding above your stop price on resume, EXIT immediately. Don't hope.
Halted in your favor
Trail your stop down. Prepare for a volatile resume. Be ready to cover quickly if it reverses.
Not yet in a trade
Wait for resume and first 1–2 candles. Halts often resume with a big directional move — let price discovery happen before entering.
RMSG Halt Lesson
RMSG halted with bid at $1.66–$1.80 while short at $1.55. The bid holding elevated during the halt was the signal to exit on resume. Waiting cost an additional $15–$20 loss vs cutting immediately.
10 — Stocks to Avoid Shorting
10 Never Short These
  • ❌
    Market cap under $5M — These can go 10x before collapsing. WOK had $292K market cap and went from $1.40 to $10. No borrow available anyway in most cases.
  • ❌
    Short interest above 25% — Crowded shorts = massive squeeze potential. UCAR had 27.4% short interest — dangerous. Always check SI before entering.
  • ❌
    Pre-earnings names — Any stock with earnings in the next 5 days. A beat sends it up 30%+ against your short. Wait until after the report.
  • ❌
    Stocks with real catalysts — Real earnings beats, real FDA approvals, real major contracts. Even if overextended, these can sustain. RCT ($30M deal), RECT (profitable, real contracts), MOBX (AMD partnership).
  • ❌
    Macro-driven moves — Oil prices, geopolitical events, Fed decisions. SCO, DLXY moved on macro. Not your edge.
  • ❌
    Large caps ($500M+) — XRX, SOUN, META, AMD. Institutional ownership, real fundamentals. Not your setup.
  • ❌
    Day after a big loss — Emotional state is compromised. Revenge trading turns one bad day into two. Take the day off.

12 — Psychology Rules
12 Trading Psychology Rules
  • 🧠
    Watch more than you trade — The best sessions have 0–1 trades. Patience is your edge. Most days the right call is no trade.
  • 🧠
    Never force a trade — If it doesn't fit the checklist, it's not a trade. There will always be another setup tomorrow.
  • 🧠
    No revenge trading — After a loss, the worst thing you can do is immediately look for a trade to "make it back." Take a break. Come back fresh.
  • 🧠
    When the answer is "both" — If you're partly excited about a trade AND partly trying to make back losses, you don't trade. Emotion overrides discipline every time.
  • 🧠
    Greed kills trades — WOK was the right thesis at the right time. Greed (not honoring the stop) turned a manageable loss into a big one. Cut when the price says cut, not when your P&L says so.
  • 🧠
    Capital preservation first — A day with no trades is better than a day with a big loss. Protect the account. The market is open every day.
  • 📊
    $3,000 daily loss limit — walk away — Your data proves this: 21 days with losses over $10K account for 54% of all your losses ($414,824). Hit $3K, close everything, step away. No exceptions.
  • 📊
    Never trade after 2:00 PM — Your trade history shows 9AM–2PM is solidly profitable. 3PM–7PM destroyed $1,301,905 in cumulative P&L. You are a morning trader. Stop at 2.
  • 📊
    Mandatory day off after $3K+ loss — 27 times in your history a big losing day was directly followed by another losing day. Revenge trading is confirmed in your data. Pause after big losses.
13 — Hard Lessons Learned
13 Real Trades, Real Lessons
RMSG — April 2026 · Shorted at $1.55, halted, exited ~$1.75. Loss: ~$100.
Lesson: Always set stop before entry, not after. Halt resumes on parabolic stocks almost always favor the direction of the move. Plan halt exit in advance.
BIRD (NewBird AI) — April 2026 · No borrow available on TOS. Missed the short from $7 to $3.
Lesson: Always check IBKR for borrow, not just TOS. IBKR has dramatically larger borrow inventory. TOS saying "unavailable" doesn't mean truly unavailable.
WOK — May 2026 · Shorted 1,000 shares at $1.40. Stock squeezed to $10 before crashing to $1.20. Big loss.
Lesson: Never short stocks with market cap under $5M ($292K market cap). Ultra-thin floats can go 10x before collapsing. 1,000 shares was too large. Stop at $1.70 was not honored. Three rules broken in one trade.
AEHL — May 2026 · Great thesis, borrow available at nominal rate. Never gave clean HOD rejection entry. Watched it all day, no trade.
Lesson: Good discipline — waiting for the entry. Not forcing a trade when price action doesn't confirm. The best trade was no trade.
RCT, RECT, MOBX — Various 2026 · Correctly identified as having real catalysts and avoided shorting.
Lesson: Catalyst quality analysis is working. Avoiding real catalysts is as important as finding weak ones.
